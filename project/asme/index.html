ASME SDC 2019

Built a bot capable of picking, storing and placing balls for the competition

Hitesh Kandala
Feb 4, 2019
Code

In my freshmen year, I was recruited in the tech team Unmesh Mashruwala Innovation Cell along with 10 other freshmen and our first project was the competition "Student Design Challenge" organised every year by American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME). The competition required us to build a remote controlled bot of maximum dimensions 50x50, capable of picking, storing and placing balls of sizes ranging from table-tennis balls to as large as basket balls.

My Contributions

  • Programmed Roboteq to control the encoder-geared DC motors used to rotate the mechanum wheels.
  • Programmed arduino to simultaneously control a stepper and 2 servo motors.
  • Controlled the whole bot using transmitter during testing phase to improve speed and accuracy.

The code used for the competition is available here

Results

After working hard for 3 months, we flew to Vellore for the Aisa-Pacific Regionals to compete with 20 other colleges from all over Asia. After 3 days of planning strategies and sleepless nights, we finally emerged as the Overall Winners of the Asia-Pacific Regionals. With this we qualified to represent India at the World Finals at Utah, USA in Nov 2019.
