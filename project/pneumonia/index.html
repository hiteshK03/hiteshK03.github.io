Pneumonia Detection

Detecting Pnuemonia regions from X-rays

Hitesh Kandala
Aug 7, 2020
Bounding Box:‘Blue’ is the ground truth and ‘Red’ is the predicted one

In summer 2020, when the whole world was caught in a pandemic, I hopefully landed an internship at Endimension Technology Pvt Ltd., a healthcare Aritificial Intelligence startup incubated at IIT Bombay.

Problem Statement:

To classify if a patient has pneumonia or not, and if he/she has then to detect regions of fluid in the chest X-rays.

Data:

The dataset consisted of images of chest X-rays of Indian patients in different formats such as Dicom, NifTI, jpg, etc.

Overview of work done:

I, first of all, had to build a pre-processing pipeline to feed images of different formats and different sizes.

I implemented a tensorflow model and experimented and optimized different object detection models and ensembled them to improve metrics.

In my one month work, I achieved sensitivity and specificity of more than 90% on the test dataset, consisting of over 3000 images.

Note : The actual deep learning architecture used is confidential as per company policy.
